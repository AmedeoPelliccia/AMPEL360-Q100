# H₂ Hazard Symbol Guidelines

## Purpose

This directory contains design guidelines and specifications for hydrogen-specific hazard symbols used in the AMPEL360 Q100 liquid hydrogen propulsion system.

## Symbol Categories

### Cryogenic Hazards
- **H2-CRYO-001:** Extreme cold (-253°C) liquid hydrogen
- **H2-BOIL-001:** Boil-off rate and venting warnings

### Flammability Hazards
- **H2-FLAM-001:** Flammable hydrogen (4-75% mixture range in air)
- **H2-LEAK-001:** Hydrogen leak detection active

### Pressure Hazards
- **H2-PRES-001:** High pressure (up to 700 bar) storage and distribution
- **H2-VENT-001:** Active pressure venting operations

### Atmospheric Hazards
- **H2-ASPH-001:** Asphyxiation risk from oxygen displacement

## Design Principles

### Color Coding
- **Cryogenic:** Cyan (#00CCFF) - represents cold/ice
- **Flammable:** Red/Orange (#FF3300) - standard fire hazard
- **Pressure:** Yellow (#FFAA00) - caution/warning
- **Asphyxiation:** Purple/Gray - breathing hazard

### Shape Standards
- **Triangle:** Warning (general hazard)
- **Diamond:** Flammable materials (NFPA/GHS standard)
- **Circle:** Mandatory action required
- **Square:** Information/equipment location

## Integration with ATA 28

These symbols integrate with ATA 28 (Fuel Systems) documentation:
- **28-10:** Storage (cryogenic tanks, pressure vessels)
- **28-20:** Distribution (lines, valves, manifolds)
- **28-30:** Dump system (emergency venting)
- **28-40:** Indicating (sensors, gauges, warnings)

## Certification Requirements

All H₂ hazard symbols must:
1. Be approved by safety engineering (STK_SAF)
2. Pass human factors usability testing
3. Comply with EASA CS-25 and FAA Part 25 requirements
4. Be validated with maintenance and flight crews
5. Maintain visibility in all lighting conditions (day/night/emergency)
